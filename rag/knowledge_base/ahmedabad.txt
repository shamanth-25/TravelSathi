City: Ahmedabad

Spot: Sabarmati Ashram
Sabarmati Ashram, located on the banks of the Sabarmati River, was one of the primary residences of Mahatma Gandhi, who lived here for twelve years during India's freedom struggle. It served as the starting point for the historic Dandi Salt March in 1930. The ashram houses a museum (Gandhi Smarak Sangrahalaya) containing personal artifacts, letters, and photographs of Gandhi, as well as Hriday Kunj, the cottage where he lived, offering visitors a serene and historically rich experience.

Spot: Adalaj Stepwell
Adalaj Stepwell (Adalaj ni Vav) is a unique Hindu-Islamic architectural marvel built in 1498 by Queen Rudabai to serve as a water reservoir and resting place. It is a five-story deep stepwell with intricate carvings of mythological scenes, foliage, and Islamic geometric patterns. Its underground chambers are designed to stay significantly cooler than the outside temperature, and the sandstone structure represents a peak of medieval craftsmanship.

Festival: Uttarayan (Kite Festival) & Navratri
Uttarayan is the International Kite Festival celebrated in Ahmedabad on January 14th and 15th to mark the transition of winter to summer. The city's skies fill with millions of colorful kites, and family gatherings take place on rooftops with loud music and local food like Undhiyu. Navratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to Goddess Durga, featuring vibrant evening Garba and Dandiya Raas traditional folk dances performed in colorful traditional dresses.

Cuisine: Gujarati Thali & Dhokla
Ahmedabad is famous for its sweet-and-savory vegetarian cuisine. A Gujarati Thali is an elaborate meal containing flatbreads (rotlo), vegetable curries, dal, kadhi, rice, and snacks (farsan) like Khaman Dhokla (spongy steamed gram flour cakes) and Khandvi. Manek Chowk is a bustling city square that transforms into a lively night street food market, famous for local specialties like Gwalior Dosa, heavy cheese sandwiches, and local Kulfi ice cream.

Language & Phrases: Gujarati
Gujarati is the official and most widely spoken language in Ahmedabad, alongside Hindi and English.
Common Gujarati phrases for travelers:
- Hello: "નમસ્તે" (Namaste) / "જય શ્રી કૃષ્ણ" (Jai Shri Krishna)
- Thank you: "આભાર" (Aabhar)
- How are you?: "કેમ છો?" (Kem cho?)
- I am fine: "મજામાં" (Majaama)
- How much is this?: "આ કેટલાનું છે?" (Aa ketlaanu chhe?)
- Please: "મહેરબાની કરીને" (Maherbani karine)
- Yes: "હા" (Haa)
- No: "ના" (Naa)
- Goodbye / See you: "આવજો" (Aavjo)
- Where is...?: "... ક્યાં છે?" (... kyaa chhe?)
- Very good: "ખૂબ સરસ" (Khoob saras)
- Water: "પાણી" (Paani)
