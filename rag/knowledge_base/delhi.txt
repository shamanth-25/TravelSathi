City: Delhi

Spot: Red Fort & Qutub Minar
The Red Fort is a historic fort in Old Delhi, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638 when he decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi. It represents the pinnacle of Mughal architectural style, combining Persian, Timurid, and Hindu design elements. Qutub Minar, located in South Delhi, is a 73-meter-tall minaret built in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak after the defeat of Delhi's last Hindu kingdom. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring beautiful red sandstone carvings and inscriptions.

Spot: India Gate & Humayun's Tomb
India Gate is a memorial arch located along the Rajpath in New Delhi, built to honor the British Indian Army soldiers who died in the First World War. Beneath the arch burns the Amar Jawan Jyoti (eternal flame). Humayun's Tomb, built in 1570, was the first garden-tomb on the Indian subcontinent, commissioned by Humayun's chief consort Bega Begum. Its design directly inspired the architecture of the Taj Mahal.

Festival: Diwali & Holi
Diwali, the festival of lights, is celebrated with immense joy in Delhi, featuring decorated markets, illuminated houses with clay lamps, colorful rangoli, family prayers, and exchange of traditional sweets. Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with organic colors, water guns, community feasts, music, and local delicacies like gujiya. These festivals highlight the rich cultural heritage and community spirit of the national capital.

Cuisine: Street Food & Mughlai Dishes
Delhi is a paradise for food lovers, famous for street food like Chole Bhature (spicy chickpeas with fried flatbread) in Karol Bagh and spicy Aloo Tikki and Chaat in Chandni Chowk. Old Delhi's Jama Masjid area is renowned for its rich Mughlai cuisine, featuring slow-cooked Butter Chicken, succulent kebabs, sheermal, and biryani. Paranthe Wali Gali in Chandni Chowk is famous for serving a wide variety of deep-fried, stuffed flatbreads (paranthas).

Language & Phrases: Hindi
Hindi is the official and most widely spoken language in Delhi, alongside English, Punjabi, and Urdu.
Common Hindi phrases for travelers:
- Hello: "नमस्ते" (Namaste)
- Thank you: "धन्यवाद" (Dhanyavaad)
- How are you?: "आप कैसे हैं?" (Aap kaise hain?)
- I am fine: "मैं ठीक हूँ" (Main theek hoon)
- How much is this?: "यह कितने का है?" (Yeh kitne ka hai?)
- Please: "कृपया" (Kripya)
- Yes: "हाँ" (Haan)
- No: "नहीं" (Nahi)
- Goodbye / See you: "अलविदा / फिर मिलेंगे" (Alvida / Phir milenge)
- Where is...?: "... कहाँ है?" (... kahan hai?)
- Very good: "बहुत अच्छा" (Bahut achha)
- Water: "पानी" (Paani)
