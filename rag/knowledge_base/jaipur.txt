City: Jaipur

Spot: Amer Fort
Amer Fort (often called Amber Fort) is a magnificent fortress located on a hill in Amer, 11 kilometers from Jaipur, Rajasthan. Originally built by Raja Man Singh I in 1592 and later expanded by Mirza Raja Jai Singh and Sawai Jai Singh, the fort is renowned for its artistic Hindu style elements. Constructed of red sandstone and marble, it overlooks the beautiful Maota Lake. Inside, key attractions include the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), the Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), the Ganesh Pol gate decorated with frescoes, and the world-famous Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), whose walls are inlaid with thousands of convex mirror pieces that illuminate the entire room with a single candle.

Spot: Hawa Mahal
Hawa Mahal, or the "Palace of Breeze", is an extraordinary five-storey pyramidal monument built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Designed by Lal Chand Ustad, the palace is constructed of red and pink-colored sandstone, blending with the theme of the "Pink City". The unique facade features 953 small casements or windows called "jharokhas", decorated with intricate latticework (jali). This architectural design allowed cool air to circulate throughout the palace during hot summers, while enabling royal women to observe daily street life and processions below without being seen from the outside.

Festival: Teej
Teej is one of the most vibrant and significant festivals celebrated in Rajasthan, especially in Jaipur. It marks the onset of the monsoon season and celebrates the holy union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. The festival is marked by women wearing traditional green and red attire, applying henna (mehendi) on their hands, singing traditional songs, and enjoying decorated swings hung from trees. A grand royal Teej procession, featuring an idol of Goddess Parvati, traditional folk dancers, musicians, decorated elephants, and camels, is taken through the streets of the Old City of Jaipur. The special sweet "Ghevar" is widely prepared and eaten during this festival.

Cuisine: Rajasthani Cuisine
Rajasthani cuisine is known for its rich flavors, use of ghee, and dishes suited for the dry desert climate:
- Dal Baati Churma: The signature dish of Rajasthan, consisting of baked whole-wheat flour balls (Baati), a spicy mix of five lentils (Dal), and a sweet powdered wheat dessert cooked with ghee and sugar (Churma).
- Gatte ki Sabji: A popular curry made with steamed gram flour (besan) roundels cooked in a rich, spicy yogurt-based gravy.
- Laal Maas: A fiery, traditional mutton curry cooked in a yogurt base and seasoned with hot Mathania red chilies of Rajasthan.
- Ker Sangri: A unique desert delicacy made from dried desert beans (Ker) and berries (Sangri) stir-fried with local spices.

Language & Phrases: Hindi & Rajasthani
While Hindi is the primary official language, the local dialect Dhundhari (a branch of Rajasthani) is widely spoken.
Common phrases for travelers:
- Hello: "नमस्ते" (Namaste) or "राम राम" (Ram Ram - local greeting)
- Thank you: "धन्यवाद" (Dhanyavad)
- How are you?: "आप कैसे हैं?" (Aap kaise hain? - Hindi) or "काँई हाल चाल है?" (Kain haal chaal hai? - Rajasthani)
- How much is this?: "यह कितने का है?" (Yeh kitne ka hai? - Hindi) or "यो कतरो को है?" (Yo katro ko hai? - Rajasthani)
- Yes: "हाँ" (Haan)
- No: "नहीं" (Nahi)
- Where is the palace?: "महल कहाँ है?" (Mahal kahan hai?)
- Water: "पानी" (Paani)
- Goodbye: "नमस्ते" (Namaste)
