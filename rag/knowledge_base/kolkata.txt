City: Kolkata

Spot: Victoria Memorial
The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Central Kolkata, built between 1906 and 1921. It is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria, Empress of India, and is now a museum under the Ministry of Culture. The building is designed in the Indo-Saracenic revivalist style, using white Makrana marble (the same marble used in the Taj Mahal). The memorial is surrounded by lush, manicured gardens, water bodies, and houses a rich collection of colonial era paintings, sculptures, and historic manuscripts.

Spot: Howrah Bridge
Howrah Bridge is a balanced cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River in West Bengal, linking Kolkata and Howrah. Commissioned in 1943, it was originally named the New Howrah Bridge and was renamed Rabindra Setu in 1965 in honor of Rabindranath Tagore. It carries a heavy daily flow of vehicles and pedestrians, and is one of the busiest cantilever bridges in the world. Its majestic steel frame serves as the definitive symbol and gateway of Kolkata.

Festival: Durga Puja
Durga Puja is the grandest and most culturally significant festival in Kolkata, celebrated to worship Goddess Durga and commemorate her victory over Mahishasura. The festival spans several days, transforming the entire city into an open-air art gallery with thousands of elaborate, thematic pandals housing beautiful clay idols of the Goddess. The festival features dynamic drumming (dhaak), traditional dance, ethnic dress, and pandal-hopping all night, concluding with the immersion of the idols in the Hooghly River.

Cuisine: Bengali Sweets & Fish Curry
Kolkata is famous for its sweet confectionery, particularly Rasgulla (spongy cottage cheese balls soaked in sugar syrup) and Mishti Doi (sweet fermented yogurt). Macher Jhol is a traditional spicy Bengali fish curry cooked in mustard oil, seasoned with Panch Phoron (five-spice blend), and served with steaming white rice. Kolkata Kathi Rolls (skewered meat wrapped in paratha bread) are also a highly popular street food across the city.

Language & Phrases: Bengali
Bengali is the official and most widely spoken language in Kolkata, alongside English and Hindi.
Common Bengali phrases for travelers:
- Hello: "নমস্কার" (Nomoshkar)
- Thank you: "ধন্যবাদ" (Dhonnobad)
- How are you?: "কেমন আছেন?" (Kemon achen?)
- I am fine: "আমি ভালো আছি" (Ami bhalo achi)
- How much is this?: "এটার দাম কত?" (Etar dam koto?)
- Please: "দয়া করে" (Doya kore)
- Yes: "হ্যাঁ" (Hya)
- No: "না" (Na)
- Goodbye / See you: "আবার দেখা হবে" (Abar dekha hobe)
- Where is...?: "... কোথায়?" (... kothay?)
- Very good: "খুব ভালো" (Khub bhalo)
- Water: "জল" (Jol)
