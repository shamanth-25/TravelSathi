City: Varanasi

Spot: Kashi Vishwanath Temple
The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples, dedicated to Lord Shiva (Vishwanath, meaning Ruler of the Universe). Located in the ancient holy city of Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, on the western bank of the sacred river Ganga, it is home to one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (holiest Shiva temples). The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times throughout history; the current structure was constructed by the Maratha monarch Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore in 1780. Maharaja Ranjit Singh of the Sikh Empire donated gold to plate the temple's spires, giving it the name "Golden Temple of Varanasi".

Spot: Ganga Aarti
Ganga Aarti is a spectacular and deeply spiritual daily evening ritual performed on the banks of the River Ganga, most famously at the Dashashwamedh Ghat in Varanasi. Young priests clad in saffron robes perform the ritual using heavy, multi-layered brass lamps, incense sticks, and flowers, synchronized to the rhythmic chanting of Vedic hymns and the ringing of bells. Thousands of pilgrims and tourists gather on the steps of the ghats and on boats in the river to watch the spectacular ceremony, which pays reverence to the river goddess Ganga and Lord Shiva.

Festival: Diwali and Dev Deepawali
While Diwali, the festival of lights, is celebrated with grand joy across Varanasi, the city hosts a unique and magical festival called Dev Deepawali ("Diwali of the Gods") exactly fifteen days after Diwali, on the full moon night of Kartik Poornima (November). According to Hindu mythology, the gods descend to Earth to bathe in the holy Ganges. On this night, every single steps of all 84 ghats in Varanasi are illuminated with millions of flickering clay lamps (diyas). The visual spectacle is accompanied by prayers, music, and floating lamps on the river.

Language & Phrases: Hindi
Hindi is the primary language spoken in Varanasi, along with the local dialect Bhojpuri.
Common Hindi phrases for travelers:
- Hello: "नमस्ते" (Namaste)
- Thank you: "धन्यवाद" (Dhanyavad) or "शुक्रिया" (Shukriya)
- How are you?: "आप कैसे हैं?" (Aap kaise hain?)
- I am fine: "मैं ठीक हूँ" (Main theek hoon)
- How much is this?: "यह कितने का है?" (Yeh kitne ka hai?)
- Yes: "हाँ" (Haan)
- No: "नहीं" (Nahi)
- Where is...?: "... कहाँ है?" (... kahan hai?)
- Water: "पानी" (Paani)
- Food: "खाना" (Khana)
- Goodbye: "अलविदा" (Alvida) or "नमस्ते" (Namaste)

Cultural Etiquette
Varanasi is a sacred city with deep-rooted traditions. Visitors should respect local customs:
- Modest Dress Code: When visiting temples or religious sites, dress modestly by covering shoulders, chest, and knees. Avoid sleeveless tops and shorts.
- Footwear: Always remove shoes, sandals, and leather items before entering temple premises, shrines, or home altars.
- Right Hand Use: Use your right hand to give or receive food, money, or religious offerings, as the left hand is traditionally considered unclean.
- Photography: Always ask for permission before photographing local people, especially sadhus (holy men), pilgrims, or funeral pyres at the cremation ghats (Harishchandra and Manikarnika Ghats, where photography is strictly prohibited).
- Temple entry: Some inner sanctums may have specific rules for non-Hindus or foreigners; always check signs or ask temple guides.
